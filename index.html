Education in Developing Countries

More than 80% of children and adolescents wordlwide who are not learning enough to meet minimum proficiency levels come from low- and lower-middle-income countries.

93% of secondary school-age adolescents in low-income countries will not achieve minimum proficiency levels in reading.

44% of girls and 34% of boys (10 to 19 years old) from the poorest families have never attended school or dropped out before completing primary education.

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.”
-Nelson Mandela
Education is an important part in everyone’s life. It not only makes us more knowledgeable, but it can boost a country’s economy as well. A lack in education means that people are not informed about basic health, math, and literature. Due to the shortage of funding and materials in developing countries, many students do not receive adequate schooling. In these nations, education is not their priority because of the high costs to maintain it. Most parents in developing nations make their children go to work to earn money, thinking that school is a waste of time. However, these developing nations deserve to have the same education that first-world countries have.
